MARVEL STUDIOS
By: RYAN PALOMO

Marvel Comics, founded in 1939 by Martin Goodman as Timely Publications, rose to prominence with characters like the Human Torch and Sub-Mariner in Marvel Comics #1. In the 1940s, the company gained fame with Captain America but shifted focus to crime and horror after the superhero boom faded. Rebranded as Atlas Comics in the 1950s, it found its true identity as Marvel in the 1960s under Stan Lee, who, alongside artists like Jack Kirby and Steve Ditko, created iconic characters such as Spider-Man, the Fantastic Four, Iron Man, and the X-Men. This innovative, relatable approach to superheroes revolutionized the genre. By the 1970s, Marvel expanded into television, film, and licensing, eventually establishing Marvel Studios in the 2000s, which launched the globally successful Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU), solidifying Marvel as a cultural and entertainment powerhouse.
